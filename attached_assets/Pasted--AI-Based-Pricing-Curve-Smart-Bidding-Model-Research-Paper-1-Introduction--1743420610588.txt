# **AI-Based Pricing Curve & Smart Bidding Model: Research Paper**

## **1. Introduction**
### **1.1 Purpose**
This research paper outlines the methodology behind **AI-powered commodity pricing curves and automated smart bidding** for agricultural trade. The goal is to create a **standardized pricing benchmark**, similar to used-car valuation models, while ensuring **dynamic trade execution** via a smart bidding system.

### **1.2 Problem Statement**
- Commodity pricing in India is **fragmented, opaque, and inefficient**.
- **Banks struggle to finance warehouse receipts** due to a lack of standardized pricing models.
- **Farmers and traders lack transparency**, leading to unfair deals and long negotiations.
- **Existing commodity exchanges** rely on outdated bid-match mechanisms with limited AI intervention.

## **2. Data Sources & Market References**
### **2.1 Domestic & Global Data Sources**
- **Agmarknet & APMC Data**: Government price discovery platform for mandi-level prices.
- **Spot & Futures Market Feeds**: NCDEX, MCX, CME (Chicago Mercantile Exchange).
- **Global Commodity Price Indices**: Platts, FAO price indices.
- **Trade Logs & Historical Transactions**: Internal platform trade data.

### **2.2 Learning from Used-Car Pricing Models**
- **Cars24, OLX Autos & Spinny** standardize car valuations based on age, mileage, and condition.
- Similarly, our system will build **standardized commodity pricing** based on quality, location, and market trends.

## **3. Pricing Curve Design**
### **3.1 Standardization of Commodities**
For each commodity, a **reference grade** is established to act as the **pricing benchmark**:
- **Wheat (Grade A)**: 12% moisture, 1% foreign matter.
- **Pulses (Tur Dal Grade 1)**: <10% broken grains, <2% foreign matter.
- **Oilseeds (Mustard Grade A)**: 40% oil content minimum.

## **4. AI-Driven Bidding Mechanism**
### **4.1 Standardized Smart Bidding Flow**
1. **Buyers bid on the standard reference grade** rather than individual quality variations.
2. Once matched, a **quality inspection is conducted** at the warehouse.
3. The **final settlement price adjusts dynamically** based on inspection results.
4. Both parties **see real-time adjustments** via AI-calculated delta values.

### **4.2 Dynamic Adjustments Based on AI & ML**
- **Regression Analysis & Time-Series Models** predict commodity price movement.
- **Neural Networks** identify demand surges and price anomalies.
- **Reinforcement Learning** fine-tunes bidding algorithms for optimal pricing strategies.
